About Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport

Nigeria premier international gateway, connecting Abuja to the world with world-class facilities and exceptional service.

Our Story

Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport (IATA: ABV, ICAO: DNAA), named after Nigeria first President, Dr. Nnamdi Azikiwe, is the main airport serving Abuja, Nigeria capital city. Located approximately 20 kilometers west of the city center, it serves as a vital hub connecting Nigeria to domestic and international destinations.

Since its inauguration in 1982, the airport has undergone significant expansions and modernizations to accommodate the growing number of passengers and ensure world-class service standards. The new international terminal, opened in 2018, features state-of-the-art facilities designed to enhance passenger experience.

Today, Nnamdi Azikiwe International Airport handles over 8 million passengers annually, with flights to more than 40 destinations served by 15+ airlines.
